Deprivation

Socio-economic deprivation can be defined as a state of disadvantage experienced by individuals, groups, or families whose access to goods, conveniences, and available social capital is limited relative to their local community or wider society.


IMD (Index of Multiple Deprivation)

The Indices of Deprivation are unique measures of deprivation at a small local area level (Lower-layer Super Output Areas) across England, that have been produced by the Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities (DLUHC) and its predecessors since 2000.

The DLUHC measure deprivation in a broad way to encompass a wide range of an individualâ€™s living conditions and, as such, seven distinct domains of deprivation are used:

  • Income
  • Employment
  • Education, Skills and Training
  • Health Deprivation and Disability
  • Crime
  • Barriers to Housing and Services
  • Living Environment

Each of these measures are based on a unique basket of indicators which can be recognised and measured separately, with the domains then combined to produce an overall relative measure of deprivation - the Index of Multiple Deprivation.
